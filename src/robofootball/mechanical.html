Mechanical Overview

The mechanical engineering majors on the team were tasked with designing and building all eleven of the robots on the team. They have improved on designs of old and engineered entirely new robots to play for the team this year.


Design Details

The mechanical engineers on this team were required to design 6 new robots from the ground up, as well as make improvements to the 5 that were kept from last year. This was completed through a systematic process of concept design, manufacturing, testing, and redesign.



These team members began the process by using SolidWorks to draw up concepts of the robots. The models could be manipulated and tested to find any errors or weak points. For a competitive team, individual robots had to be designed to exceed at their specific function. The 6 new robots were the quarterback, center, kicker, running back, and 2 wide receivers. This called for a lot of brainstorming and idea-generation. Certain functions were replicated from the designs of previous years, and others were entirely new. Servos and electric motors were employed to complete all required functions; including swinging arms, sliding pins, opening and closing a gripper, and rotating launch wheels.


When the models were satisfactory, each part was generated into a 2-dimensional contour code that was used in the machine shop. This code allowed for precision cuts on a CNC table, and created exact parts again and again out of high density plastic. When all parts were cut for a specific robot, assembly began. The engineers ensured that all parts fit together exactly, so that the robots would run just as well as they were designed. Countless hours were spent sanding edges and measuring to guarantee perfect operation. Once a robot was completely assembled, it was handed off to the electrical engineers for wiring.
